Game Summary

Collab is a 2-player puzzle platforming game where you need to work together to solve various puzzles. Each player can switch their character between four different forms: normal mode, boost mode, shrink mode, and swap mode. Normal mode is the default state of the characters, boost mode allows the player to jump higher, shrink mode allows the player to shrink and fit into smaller gaps, and swap mode allows the players to switch positions. Using these character forms, the players need to navigate around various obstacles like doors, pits, and high ledges. There is a twist, however, that makes the puzzles a lot more challenging. Both players have a combined limit to how many times they can switch forms, so they need to be careful and smart about which forms they switch into. Both players need to reach the end of the level to complete it!

This game was created as a personal side project, just to say I made a complete game in Unity. I did get some help from my friend Joseph Ferrugio who made the song present in the game, but other than that I did it entirely on my own. This game really helped me understand Unity and C# and game me a lot of confidence in my game creating abilities.

Thoughts

I really enjoyed creating this game and still look back on it as one of my best projects I have ever created. I find the puzzle mechanic to be especially unique and fun to mess around with. The movement and animations in the game I also think are very smooth and satisfying, which adds to the charm of the game. The game took me about 2 months to make in full, and I went from very little Unity experience to getting a significant chunk of the game development process. At the time I had plans to extend the game and take it further, where I would flesh out the sprites and make the game much longer, maybe even adding in a story. However, now I think it should be left in the past as a piece of my game development history.
